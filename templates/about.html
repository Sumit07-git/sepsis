About This System

Understanding sepsis prediction and the AI model

What is Sepsis?

Sepsis is a life-threatening condition that occurs when the body's response to infection causes widespread inflammation, leading to tissue damage, organ failure, and potentially death. It is one of the leading causes of mortality in intensive care units worldwide.

48.9 Million

Global sepsis cases annually

11 Million

Sepsis-related deaths per year

Early Detection

Critical for survival

Clinical Scoring Systems

SOFA Score

Sequential Organ Failure Assessment

Evaluates dysfunction across multiple organ systems:

  • Respiratory: Based on oxygen saturation
  • Cardiovascular: Mean arterial pressure
  • Renal: Creatinine levels
  • Coagulation: Platelet count

Score Range: 0-8+ (Higher = More severe)

SIRS Criteria

Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome

Counts presence of these criteria:

  • Temperature: <36°C or >38°C
  • Heart Rate: >90 bpm
  • Respiratory Rate: >20/min
  • WBC: <4 or >12 ×10³/μL

Count: 0-4 (≥2 suggests SIRS)

qSOFA Score

Quick SOFA

Rapid bedside assessment:

  • Respiratory Rate: ≥22/min
  • Altered Mentation: GCS <15
  • Systolic BP: ≤100 mmHg

Score: 0-3 (≥2 suggests sepsis)

Machine Learning Model

Algorithm

Random Forest Classifier

  • Ensemble of 200 decision trees
  • Trained on 2,000 patient records
  • Balanced with SMOTE technique
  • Class-weighted for rare events

Features

32 Engineered Features

  • 11 base clinical measurements
  • 4 SOFA score components
  • 4 SIRS criteria flags
  • 8 binary risk indicators
  • 5 composite risk scores

Performance

Validation Metrics

  • High accuracy on test set
  • Balanced precision and recall
  • Strong ROC-AUC score
  • Validated with cross-validation

Risk Classification

VERY HIGH RISK

Probability: ≥70%

Critical sepsis indicators. Multiple organ dysfunction. Requires immediate intervention and ICU-level care.

HIGH RISK

Probability: 50-69%

Elevated sepsis risk. Multiple concerning indicators. Urgent clinical review and close monitoring required.

MODERATE RISK

Probability: 30-49%

Some concerning indicators present. Enhanced monitoring and frequent reassessment recommended.

LOW RISK

Probability: <30%

Minimal sepsis indicators. Continue routine monitoring and standard care protocols.

Dataset Information

PhysioNet Sepsis Challenge Dataset

The model is trained on data inspired by the PhysioNet/Computing in Cardiology Challenge 2019 dataset, which contains ICU patient records with hourly vital signs and laboratory values.
